About

I am a PhD student in Mathematics (Probability/Statistics) at Imperial College London, supervised by Prof. R. Passeggeri. My research focuses on random measures and stochastic PDEs. My position is fully funded by the Roth Scholarship.

Previously, I obtained my MSc in Mathematics from the University of Udine, where I also attended the "Toppo Wasserman" School of Advanced Studies, a selective excellence programme modelled after the French grandes écoles. My MSc thesis, Bounds for exotic options, applied Villani's optimal transport theory to path-dependent option pricing, completed during a research stay at Zeliade Systems in Paris.

I currently serve as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for the undergraduate course Calculus and Applications at Imperial.

Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of probability theory and measure theory. I am particularly interested in the structure and properties of random measures and their extensions to infinite-dimensional spaces, as well as stochastic PDEs and optimal transport in measure spaces.
